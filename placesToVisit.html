Periyar National Park

Must Visit
4.7 /5

2km from city centre

Spread across an area of 300 square miles, Periyar National Park is covered by lush evergreen and deciduous forests.

It serves as home to various animals such as deer, elephants, sambar, Nilgiri Langurs, etc.

Periyar Tiger Trail

Must Visit
4.5 /5

2km from city centre

Tourists trek along the Tiger Trail and thick vegetation of the reserve along with guides and Forest Department officials.

It is an experience and worth remembering. Tourists can also spot elephants, sambhars, and tigers there.

Bamboo Rafting

Must Try
4.2 /5

1km from city centre

Bamboo Rafting is a perfect activity for all the water sports enthusiasts. The facility is offered by the Forest Department.

At Thekkady one can enjoy this activity in the catchment area of the national park.

Thekkady Lake

Must Visit
4.0 /5

5km from city centre

Tucked in the heart of the jungle, Thekkady or Periyar Lake is famous for its wide varieties of wildlife. You can also take a boat ride here.

It is this reservoir that provides sufficient water to the nearby areas and the forest.

Anakkara

3.8 /5

2km from city centre

Mudra Cultural Centre

Must Visit
4.0 /5

1km from city centre

Mudra Cultural Centre is famous for cultural events that specifically involve the traditional dance form, Kathakali and India's oldest form of martial arts, Kalari.

Tourists can also witness the laborious preparations the artists have to make before their performances.

Read more

Kadathanadan Kalari Centre

3.8 /5

1km from city centre

Kadathanadan Kalari Centre is a renowned centre of the traditional form of martial arts, Kalaripayyattu which was invented by Sage Parshuram.

The programs arranged by the centre are specifically to showcase the ancient form of self-defence .

Read more

Chellarkovil View Point

3.6 /5

8km from city centre

Chellarkovil Viewpoint is famous for a stunning view of hilly terrains covered in lush green vegetation and an enchanting waterfall.

The viewpoint has a watchtower with a telescope installed on a terrace which allows tourists to get a splendid view .

Vandiperiyar

3.9 /5

9km from city centre

Vandeperiyar is a quaint, little town in Idukki District, Kerala, 18km from Thekkady. At an altitude of 2743ft above sea level, this hilly town is cool.

It is a delight for travellers who enjoy exploring plantations and spice farms. .

Pandikuzhi

3.8 /5

3km from city centre

Pandikuzhi is a humble little village famous for its picturesque beauty. Its captivating natural landscape, the thrilling terrain, a pristine stream of water flowing.

It flows through the valley covered with lush green foliage makes Pandikuzhi is a famous picnic destination.

Jungle Night Patrol

Must Try
4.2 /5

3km from city centre

Jungle Night Patrol is one of the top thrilling things to do in Thekkady. It is a trekking activity which begins at two points which are:-

Periyar National Park- Entrance , Gate of the Periyar Tiger Reserve and from the Bamboo Grooves. The activity is performed under supervision .

Nature Walks is another one of the top things to do in Thekkady and a very popular activity in the region.

Walking through the stunning wildlife and vegetation of the sleepy town, you can come across varied varieties of flora and fauna, wild animals and the like. .
